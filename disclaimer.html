Medical Disclaimer

Last updated: 2026

Bloom provides general information about pregnancy. It is not medical advice and is not a substitute for care from a qualified healthcare professional. If you have a medical concern, contact your provider. In an emergency, call your local emergency number immediately.

For information only

All content in Bloom — including week-by-week development information, size comparisons, food and drink guidance, facts, and any logging features — is provided for general educational and informational purposes only. It does not constitute medical, clinical, nutritional, or professional advice, and it does not establish any provider–patient relationship.

Always consult your provider

Every pregnancy is different. Always seek the advice of your physician, midwife, or another qualified health professional with any questions about your pregnancy, your health, or your baby's health. Never disregard professional medical advice, or delay seeking it, because of something you read in this app.

Size and development figures are averages

Length and weight figures, fruit-size comparisons, and developmental milestones are population averages drawn from public references. Individual babies vary considerably and healthily. These figures cannot tell you whether your pregnancy is progressing normally — only your provider, with proper examination and testing, can do that.

Food and drink guidance is general

The "Can I eat this?" information reflects general public guidance (from bodies such as the FDA, EPA, CDC, ACOG, Mayo Clinic, and the NHS) and is summarized for convenience. It is not personalized advice. Guidance can change, can vary by country, and may not account for your specific circumstances, allergies, or medical history. Confirm anything important with your provider.

Logging is not monitoring

Any weights, measurements, feelings, symptoms, or notes you record are for your own personal reference. Bloom does not analyze them for medical purposes, does not detect medical problems, and is not a substitute for clinical monitoring or prenatal appointments.

When to seek urgent help

This app cannot assess urgency. If you experience symptoms that concern you — such as heavy bleeding, severe pain, a significant change in your baby's movements, severe headache, or any symptom your provider has told you to watch for — contact your provider or emergency services right away. Do not rely on this app to decide whether a symptom is serious.
